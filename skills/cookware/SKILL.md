# Cookware Safety Specialist Skill

## 1. Core Assessment Rules
* Check for non-stick fluoropolymer coatings (PTFE, PFOA, GenX).
* Check for heavy metal contamination in glazes (Lead, Cadmium).
* Check for uncoated aluminum leaching risks.

## 2. California Proposition 65 Toxins Database
<details>
<summary>Click to view Cookware Toxins list</summary>

* **PFOA (Perfluorooctanoic Acid)**: Listed under California Prop 65 as causing cancer and reproductive toxicity. Banned in manufacturing but resides in older/cheaper non-stick pans. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene / Teflon)**: Polymer coating. Safe at low temps, but degrades above 500°F (260°C) releasing toxic fluorinated fumes (polymer fume fever). Verdict: **CAUTION**.
* **Lead**: Listed under Prop 65. Can leach from low-quality ceramic glazes or pewter. Causes neurological damage. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **Cadmium**: Listed under Prop 65 for cancer and reproductive harm. Used in red/orange ceramic glazes. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **Aluminum**: Can leach into acidic foods when using uncoated aluminum pans. Associated with neurotoxicity. Verdict: **CAUTION**.
</details>
