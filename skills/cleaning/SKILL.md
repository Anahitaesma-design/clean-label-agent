# Cleaning Safety Specialist Skill

## 1. Core Assessment Rules
* Check for volatile organic compounds (VOCs) that cause asthma or lung irritation.
* Check for reactive gas hazards (e.g. mixing Chlorine Bleach and Ammonia).
* Check for hormone-disrupting surfactants (Alkylphenol Ethoxylates).

## 2. EPA CompTox Database Hazards
<details>
<summary>Click to view Cleaning Hazards list</summary>

* **Sodium Hypochlorite (Chlorine Bleach)**: Strong respiratory irritant. Highly hazardous if mixed with Ammonia or Acids, releasing toxic Chlorine/Chloramine gas. Verdict: **CAUTION**.
* **Ammonia (Ammonium Hydroxide)**: Severe eye, skin, and respiratory tract irritant. Listed in EPA CompTox as high toxicity. Verdict: **CAUTION**.
* **NPEs (Nonylphenol Ethoxylates)**: EPA CompTox flags NPEs as persistent endocrine disruptors that mimic estrogen and harm aquatic life. Banned in EU cleaners. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **Phthalates (Fragrance)**: Often hidden under "Fragrance/Parfum". EPA CompTox lists them as endocrine disruptors and developmental toxins. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **Formaldehyde**: Present as preservative in some dish soaps. Known carcinogen. Verdict: **UNSAFE**.
* **Ethanolamines (MEA / DEA / TEA)**: EPA CompTox flags them for skin sensitization and potential nitrosamine carcinogen formation. Verdict: **CAUTION**.
</details>
